Diabetes Mellitus Medications that may increase body weight:
Most insulins
Sulfonylureas and Meglitinides (e.g., nateglinide, repaglinide)
Thiazolidinediones (and other medication names ending in “-zone”)
Diabetes Mellitus Medications that may decrease body weight:
Metformin
Glucagon-like peptide-1 (GLP-1) receptor agonists (e.g., “-tides”)
Dual GLP-1 and glucose-dependent insulinotropic (GIP) agonist
Sodium glucose co-transporter 2 inhibitors (e.g., “-flozins”)
Alpha glucosidase inhibitors (e.g., acarbose, miglitol)
Amylin mimetic (pramlintide) 
Diabetes Mellitus Medications that have neutral effects on body weight:
Dipeptidyl peptidase-4 (DPP4) inhibitors (e.g., “-gliptins”)
